Description

CryptoHint helps solve cryptograms. It performs a letter pattern search against a large wordlist and displays a list of words matching the letter pattern of a word from a cryptogram. The user may select a word from the list and specify individual letter guesses to specify a letter substitution map to improve subsequent word searches. Cryptogram letter pattern dictionary books exist, but CryptoHint performs the same function electronically and fits in your pocket.

Cryptograms are short pieces of text encrypted with a substitution cipher in which each letter of the alphabet is replaced by a different letter:

  • all instances of a particular letter in the original text are replaced by the same letter in the encrypted text;
  • no letter in the original text may be replaced by itself;
  • numeric digits and punctuation are unchanged.

For example, the phrase

TO BE, OR NOT TO BE, THAT IS THE QUESTION.
would be converted to a cryptogram like
AV UD, VG LVA AV UD, AXPA NO AXD IKDOANVL.

A crypto-family puzzle (also known as a crypto clan) is a list of words sharing something in common encrypted with a substitution cipher similar to cryptograms.
