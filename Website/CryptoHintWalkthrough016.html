Cryptogram Walkthrough 016

2012.11.25

YL KVU AKLKXZAKVR QZUW KG WPO DKUTN, WK AZUUJ AOZLR WK PZTBO KLO'R UYXPWR ZLN NKVMTO KLO'R NVWYOR. - ZUWPVU RSPKQOLPZVOU

KLO'R is a contraction, R is likely S, T or possibly D. AZUUJ has a double letter pair - if U were E, the author's first name has 2 E's, could it be GEORGE? No, there's only one Z in the first name; but then KVU might be THE? There are a number of K's in the cryptogram, and KG would be TO. Let's try KVU=THE and KG=TO.

YL KVU AKLKXZAKVR QZUW KG WPO DKUTN, WK AZUUJ AOZLR WK PZTBO KLO'R UYXPWR ZLN NKVMTO KLO'R NVWYOR. - ZUWPVU RSPKQOLPZVOU
** THE *T*T***TH* **E* TO *** *TE**, *T **EE* ***** *T ***** T**'* E***** *** *TH*** T**'* *H****. - *E**HE ***T*****H*E

I don't think those were good guesses. The author's first name ends in -HE, which might be unlikely, and I'm not coming up with any decent possibilities for the contraction.

Since K is very common, perhaps K=E? I'll back out all the previous guesses and let K=E. Let's see what happens.

YL KVU AKLKXZAKVR QZUW KG WPO DKUTN, WK AZUUJ AOZLR WK PZTBO KLO'R UYXPWR ZLN NKVMTO KLO'R NVWYOR. - ZUWPVU RSPKQOLPZVOU
** E** *E*E***E** **** E* *** *E***, *E ***** ***** *E ***** E**'* ****** *** *E**** E**'* ******. - ****** ***E********

I don't think that was a good choice either: KVU would be a 3-letter word starting with E, perhaps END; the contraction would be another 3-letter word starting with E. Maybe the contraction is END'S.

YL KVU AKLKXZAKVR QZUW KG WPO DKUTN, WK AZUUJ AOZLR WK PZTBO KLO'R UYXPWR ZLN NKVMTO KLO'R NVWYOR. - ZUWPVU RSPKQOLPZVOU
*N E** *ENE***E*S **** E* **D *E***, *E ***** *D*NS *E ****D END'S *****S *N* *E***D END'S ****DS. - ****** S**E*DN***D*

I don't think I'm getting anywhere. Back out those guesses as well.

I'm going to try AKLKXZAKVR in CryptoHint. CryptoHint finds 5 words; in Frequency sort: MONOGAMOUS; TELEOSTEAN; NOMOGENOUS; LIMICOLINE; TUBULATURE. So let's use AKLKXZAKVR=MONOGAMOUS, I don't know what the other words even mean!

YL KVU AKLKXZAKVR QZUW KG WPO DKUTN, WK AZUUJ AOZLR WK PZTBO KLO'R UYXPWR ZLN NKVMTO KLO'R NVWYOR. - ZUWPVU RSPKQOLPZVOU
*N OU* MONOGAMOUS *A** O* *** *O***, *O MA*** M*ANS *O *A*** ON*'S **G**S AN* *OU*** ON*'S *U***S. - A***U* S**O**N*AU**

Author's first name must be ARTHUR.

YL KVU AKLKXZAKVR QZUW KG WPO DKUTN, WK AZUUJ AOZLR WK PZTBO KLO'R UYXPWR ZLN NKVMTO KLO'R NVWYOR. - ZUWPVU RSPKQOLPZVOU
*N OUR MONOGAMOUS *ART O* TH* *OR**, TO MARR* M*ANS TO HA*** ON*'S R*GHTS AN* *OU*** ON*'S *UT**S. - ARTHUR S*HO**NHAU*R

"IN OUR MONOGAMOUS PART OF THE WORLD,..."

YL KVU AKLKXZAKVR QZUW KG WPO DKUTN, WK AZUUJ AOZLR WK PZTBO KLO'R UYXPWR ZLN NKVMTO KLO'R NVWYOR. - ZUWPVU RSPKQOLPZVOU
IN OUR MONOGAMOUS PART OF THE WORLD, TO MARR* MEANS TO HAL*E ONE'S RIGHTS AND DOU*LE ONE'S DUTIES. - ARTHUR S*HOPENHAUER

"...TO MARRY MEANS TO HALVE ONE'S RIGHTS AND DOUBLE ONE'S DUTIES." I hope the author's last name gets filled in, let's see...

YL KVU AKLKXZAKVR QZUW KG WPO DKUTN, WK AZUUJ AOZLR WK PZTBO KLO'R UYXPWR ZLN NKVMTO KLO'R NVWYOR. - ZUWPVU RSPKQOLPZVOU
IN OUR MONOGAMOUS PART OF THE WORLD, TO MARRY MEANS TO HALVE ONE'S RIGHTS AND DOUBLE ONE'S DUTIES. - ARTHUR S*HOPENHAUER

So close! I'm guessing the author's last name is SCHOPENHAUER. Doing an online search, I indeed find Arthur Schopenhauer is the author of the quote.

IN OUR MONOGAMOUS PART OF THE WORLD, TO MARRY MEANS TO HALVE ONE'S RIGHTS AND DOUBLE ONE'S DUTIES. - ARTHUR SCHOPENHAUER

Arthur Schopenhauer was a German philosopher, 1788-1860.
