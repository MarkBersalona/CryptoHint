Cryptogram Walkthrough 014

2012.09.11

YP JLKZIOZV RXN XT XNAYVLDQ JLIIYGGMM RM ULZKN HDLOXOKQ VGYKK OM XG GRM NLJW. - XDGRZD SLKNOMDS

VGYKK - last 2 letters might be -LL or -SS, as in SHALL or BLISS. JLIIYGGMM has a few double letters, -GGMM might be -EESS... SUCCESS? no, doesn't fit. YP starts the sentence, could be TO or IF: "TO something blank blank something..." If it were IF, "IF something blank blank something..." I'd expect a comma somewhere, but there isn't one.

Let's look at the name. XDGRZD... ARTHUR would fit. What the heck, I've got nothing better to try right now.

YP JLKZIOZV RXN XT XNAYVLDQ JLIIYGGMM RM ULZKN HDLOXOKQ VGYKK OM XG GRM NLJW. - XDGRZD SLKNOMDS
** ***U**U* HA* A* A*****R* *****TT** H* **U** *R**A*** *T*** ** AT TH* ****. - ARTHUR ******R*

This actually looks promising. I'll bet JLIIYGGMM is COMMITTEE and GRM=THE.

YP JLKZIOZV RXN XT XNAYVLDQ JLIIYGGMM RM ULZKN HDLOXOKQ VGYKK OM XG GRM NLJW. - XDGRZD SLKNOMDS
I* CO*UM*U* HA* A* A**I*OR* COMMITTEE HE *OU** *RO*A*** *TI** *E AT THE *OC*. - ARTHUR *O***ER*

XT is probably AN, since the next word starts with a vowel.

YP JLKZIOZV RXN XT XNAYVLDQ JLIIYGGMM RM ULZKN HDLOXOKQ VGYKK OM XG GRM NLJW. - XDGRZD SLKNOMDS
I* CO*UM*U* HA* AN A**I*OR* COMMITTEE HE *OU** *RO*A*** *TI** *E AT THE *OC*. - ARTHUR *O***ER*

"...HAS AN..." or maybe "...HAD AN..."? ULZKN might be COULD or WOULD - but C is already taken. If ULZKN=WOULD, then RXN=HAD. Let's try it.

YP JLKZIOZV RXN XT XNAYVLDQ JLIIYGGMM RM ULZKN HDLOXOKQ VGYKK OM XG GRM NLJW. - XDGRZD SLKNOMDS
I* COLUM*U* HAD AN AD*I*OR* COMMITTEE HE WOULD *RO*A*L* *TILL *E AT THE DOC*. - ARTHUR *OLD*ER*

"IF COLUMBUS HAD AN ADVISORY COMMITTEE HE WOULD PROBABLY STILL BE AT THE DOCK"

YP JLKZIOZV RXN XT XNAYVLDQ JLIIYGGMM RM ULZKN HDLOXOKQ VGYKK OM XG GRM NLJW. - XDGRZD SLKNOMDS
IF COLUMBUS HAD AN ADVISORY COMMITTEE HE WOULD PROBABLY STILL BE AT THE DOCK. - ARTHUR *OLDBER*

Only the author's last name is left. Is it GOLDBERG? First and last letters are the same, and G hasn't been used yet. Any other letters fit? Which ones are left... F, J, N, Q, X, Z - nope, only G works.

IF COLUMBUS HAD AN ADVISORY COMMITTEE HE WOULD PROBABLY STILL BE AT THE DOCK. - ARTHUR GOLDBERG

There may be a number of Arthur Goldbergs, but the author of this quote is Arthur Joseph Goldberg, associate justice of the U.S. Supreme Court, 1962-1965. He also served as U.S. ambassador to the United Nations, 1965-1968.
