Am I cheating if I use CryptoHint?

2012.09.06

I know what you mean. Part of the challenge in solving puzzles is to do it unassisted. Looking up words in a dictionary, taking advantage of any clues or hints that might be provided, peeking at the solution quickly - these detract from the satisfaction of solving the puzzle. Taking it to the extreme, if you gave a puzzle to a magic machine that solved the puzzle for you, where's the fun in that?

You can think of CryptoHint as a training tool. It certainly helped me. When I was developing and testing CryptoHint, it had been years since I last solved a cryptogram puzzle in the newspaper, and at first I was very rusty. The first several cryptograms I tried solving were difficult for me, and I used CryptoHint for many words in each cryptogram. But with further practice I started recognizing letter and word patterns again, and needed CryptoHint less often. Soon I was solving most cryptograms without CryptoHint at all.

In fact I now have the opposite problem of not needing CryptoHint when I DO want to use it! For CryptoHint's website I started writing cryptogram walkthroughs: solving a cryptogram by example; another way to put it might be solving a cryptogram with full commentary. I wanted to show some examples of how helpful CryptoHint could be with difficult cryptograms. After a dozen walkthroughs, however, I've been able to solve the cryptograms without using CryptoHint.

As a dictionary, CryptoHint can also be used as a training tool in pattern recognition. When you start learning how to solve cryptograms, you're told about the letter patterns of some common words. RFNIN, a 5-letter word with the third and fifth letters identical, is often THERE or WHERE; CryptoHint finds 460 other words that match the pattern, such as THESE, PIECE and BASIS. (It finds all 462 matching words as long as the letter substitution map is clear.) GIRNI, we are told, is usually WHICH, and perhaps in cryptograms WHICH is the most common; CryptoHint however finds many other common words like SERVE, LEAVE and FENCE which also fit the pattern. If I enter my last name, CryptoHint finds 571 matches, like EXPANSION, CHRISTMAS and LITHESOME.

Then there is the "wisdom" as the "prize" for solving cryptogram puzzles. Most cryptograms are quotations or words of wisdom. As a counter-example, a sudoku puzzle is a grid of numbers. If you "cheat" and use a "SudokuHint" app to solve a sudoku puzzle, what do you get in the end? A grid of numbers. If you're really stuck and use CryptoHint to solve a cryptogram, you might not get bragging rights for solving the puzzle unaided, but you're a little wiser.

Using CryptoHint isn't cheating - it's training and learning. You'll get better at solving cryptograms with practice: the more cryptograms you solve with or without CryptoHint, the better you'll get at recognizing letter and word patterns; you won't need CryptoHint as often.
