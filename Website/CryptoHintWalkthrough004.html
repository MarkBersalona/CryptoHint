Cryptogram Walkthrough 004

2011.07.02

WNWOU CJGKVO, KVQWNWO LVZWFG, DWWMF C LVFG VJGOCSWVJF NCBPGU TKCPBWZ RPDW C LCZLCB PB GKW MCZZWZ TWRR VX KPF AOWCFG. - RVSCB MWCOFCRR FLPGK

First word could be EVERY. Assume the single-letter word is A. 'EVERY blank, blank blank, blank A yadda yadda yadda' sounds possible. Let's try them.

WNWOU CJGKVO, KVQWNWO LVZWFG, DWWMF C LVFG VJGOCSWVJF NCBPGU TKCPBWZ RPDW C LCZLCB PB GKW MCZZWZ TWRR VX KPF AOWCFG. - RVSCB MWCOFCRR FLPGK
EVERY A....R, ...EVER ...E.., .EE.. A .... ...RA.E... VA...Y ..A..E. ...E A .A..A. .. ..E .A..E. .E.. .. ... .REA... - ...A. .EAR.A.. .....

Not bad at all! KVQWNWO might be WHOEVER. DWWMF could be NEEDS. 'EVERY blank, WHOEVER blank, NEEDS A something something' sound possible. KVQWNWO might also be HOWEVER - 'EVERY blank, HOWEVER blank, NEEDS A something something' sounds even better. I'll try HOWEVER first, hold off on NEEDS for the moment.

WNWOU CJGKVO, KVQWNWO LVZWFG, DWWMF C LVFG VJGOCSWVJF NCBPGU TKCPBWZ RPDW C LCZLCB PB GKW MCZZWZ TWRR VX KPF AOWCFG. - RVSCB MWCOFCRR FLPGK
EVERY A..HOR, HOWEVER .O.E.., .EE.. A .O.. O..RA.EO.. VA...Y .HA..E. ...E A .A..A. .. .HE .A..E. .E.. O. H.. .REA... - .O.A. .EAR.A.. ....H

Could VJGOCSWVJF be OUTRAGEOUS? 'EVERY blank, HOWEVER blank, NEEDS A blank OUTRAGEOUS something' Oh, GKW would be THE in that case. I'll try plugging in THE first.

WNWOU CJGKVO, KVQWNWO LVZWFG, DWWMF C LVFG VJGOCSWVJF NCBPGU TKCPBWZ RPDW C LCZLCB PB GKW MCZZWZ TWRR VX KPF AOWCFG. - RVSCB MWCOFCRR FLPGK
EVERY A.THOR, HOWEVER .O.E.T, .EE.. A .O.T O.TRA.EO.. VA..TY .HA..E. ...E A .A..A. .. THE .A..E. .E.. O. H.. .REA.T. - .O.A. .EAR.A.. ...TH

'EVERY AUTHOR, HOWEVER blank, NEEDS A something OUTRAGEOUS' - I'll try plugging in AUTHOR and OUTRAGEOUS, for some reason I'm thinking DWWMF might be something other than NEEDS.

WNWOU CJGKVO, KVQWNWO LVZWFG, DWWMF C LVFG VJGOCSWVJF NCBPGU TKCPBWZ RPDW C LCZLCB PB GKW MCZZWZ TWRR VX KPF AOWCFG. - RVSCB MWCOFCRR FLPGK
EVERY AUTHOR, HOWEVER .O.EST, .EE.S A .OST OUTRAGEOUS VA..TY .HA..E. ...E A .A..A. .. THE .A..E. .E.. O. H.S .REAST. - .OGA. .EARSA.. S..TH

'NEEDS A MOST OUTRAGEOUS' - VANITY? And is the author of the quote LOGAN PEARSALL SMITH? Not sure who he is but the name sounds familiar. I'll try the other words first: NEEDS, MOST and VANITY.

WNWOU CJGKVO, KVQWNWO LVZWFG, DWWMF C LVFG VJGOCSWVJF NCBPGU TKCPBWZ RPDW C LCZLCB PB GKW MCZZWZ TWRR VX KPF AOWCFG. - RVSCB MWCOFCRR FLPGK
EVERY AUTHOR, HOWEVER MO.EST, NEEDS A MOST OUTRAGEOUS VA..TY .HA..E. ..NE A MA.MA. .. THE DA..E. .E.. O. H.S .REAST. - .OGA. DEARSA.. SM.TH

Ummm, when I filled in NEEDS and MOST, the N in what would have been VANITY didn't fill in, so NCBPGU must be something else. And the author's name starts with a D instead of a P, that is, if NEEDS is correct. Maybe it's KEEPS instead of NEEDS, there's no other instance of N in the cryptogram. I'll back out the N and D in NEEDS.

WNWOU CJGKVO, KVQWNWO LVZWFG, DWWMF C LVFG VJGOCSWVJF NCBPGU TKCPBWZ RPDW C LCZLCB PB GKW MCZZWZ TWRR VX KPF AOWCFG. - RVSCB MWCOFCRR FLPGK
EVERY AUTHOR, HOWEVER MO.EST, .EE.S A MOST OUTRAGEOUS VA..TY .HA..E. ...E A MA.MA. .. THE .A..E. .E.. O. H.S .REAST. - .OGA. .EARSA.. SM.TH

'EVERY AUTHOR, HOWEVER MODEST' sounds right. and KPF must be HIS. HAS doesn't work, A has already been used; HES isn't a word and E has already been used; HOS isn't a word and O has already been used; HUS doesn't work, same reasons. Plug in for MODEST and HIS:

WNWOU CJGKVO, KVQWNWO LVZWFG, DWWMF C LVFG VJGOCSWVJF NCBPGU TKCPBWZ RPDW C LCZLCB PB GKW MCZZWZ TWRR VX KPF AOWCFG. - RVSCB MWCOFCRR FLPGK
EVERY AUTHOR, HOWEVER MODEST, .EE.S A MOST OUTRAGEOUS VA.ITY .HAI.ED .I.E A MADMA. I. THE .ADDED .E.. O. HIS .REAST. - .OGA. .EARSA.. SMITH

'...KEEPS A MOST OUTRAGEOUS VANITY' - modesty and vanity suggest opposites, something one might expect in a cryptogram. Didn't I consider VANITY before? Right, when I thought it was NEEDS instead of KEEPS. CHAINED has an N, would that work? Yes, B=N. Plug in for KEEPS, VANITY and CHAINED:

WNWOU CJGKVO, KVQWNWO LVZWFG, DWWMF C LVFG VJGOCSWVJF NCBPGU TKCPBWZ RPDW C LCZLCB PB GKW MCZZWZ TWRR VX KPF AOWCFG. - RVSCB MWCOFCRR FLPGK
EVERY AUTHOR, HOWEVER MODEST, KEEPS A MOST OUTRAGEOUS VANITY CHAINED .IKE A MADMAN IN THE PADDED CE.. O. HIS .REAST. - .OGAN PEARSA.. SMITH

'CHAINED LIKE A MADMAN IN THE PADDED CELL OF HIS BREAST'

WNWOU CJGKVO, KVQWNWO LVZWFG, DWWMF C LVFG VJGOCSWVJF NCBPGU TKCPBWZ RPDW C LCZLCB PB GKW MCZZWZ TWRR VX KPF AOWCFG. - RVSCB MWCOFCRR FLPGK
EVERY AUTHOR, HOWEVER MODEST, KEEPS A MOST OUTRAGEOUS VANITY CHAINED LIKE A MADMAN IN THE PADDED CELL OF HIS BREAST. - LOGAN PEARSALL SMITH

Good, the author's name got filled in with all that. Logan Pearsall Smith: according to Wikipedia, an American-born essayist and critic. American-born, not American? Born in Millville, NJ, 1865, died 1946, ah, grew up in part in England and became a British citizen in 1913.

EVERY AUTHOR, HOWEVER MODEST, KEEPS A MOST OUTRAGEOUS VANITY CHAINED LIKE A MADMAN IN THE PADDED CELL OF HIS BREAST. - LOGAN PEARSALL SMITH
