Cryptogram Walkthrough 011

2012.08.31

NBL ELAN SLVN NBDEP NH SLDEP RDNNC DV NH SL WSML NH OYHNL WEHNBLF'V RDN. - IBFDVNDWE ELVNLMM SHKLL

Sentences, particularly cryptograms, often start with THE. If NBL is THE, then the 3 instances of NH would be TO. Let's try both.

NBL ELAN SLVN NBDEP NH SLDEP RDNNC DV NH SL WSML NH OYHNL WEHNBLF'V RDN. - IBFDVNDWE ELVNLMM SHKLL
THE *E*T *E*T TH*** TO *E*** **TT* ** TO *E ***E TO **OTE **OTHE*'* **T. - *H***T*** *E*TE** *O*EE

Looks promising. Maybe it starts out "THE BEST blank blank TO..."? And RDNNC might end in Y, like PRETTY, though it doesn't have enough letters for PRETTY. DV might be IS, as in "... IS TO..." - and if so, then the letter following the apostrophe would be S, as in "BROTHER'S". I'm going to try DV=IS, it looks the most promising right now.

NBL ELAN SLVN NBDEP NH SLDEP RDNNC DV NH SL WSML NH OYHNL WEHNBLF'V RDN. - IBFDVNDWE ELVNLMM SHKLL
THE *E*T *EST THI** TO *EI** *ITT* IS TO *E ***E TO **OTE **OTHE*'S *IT. - *H*ISTI** *ESTE** *O*EE

I'm pretty sure the author's first name is CHRISTIAN.

NBL ELAN SLVN NBDEP NH SLDEP RDNNC DV NH SL WSML NH OYHNL WEHNBLF'V RDN. - IBFDVNDWE ELVNLMM SHKLL
THE NE*T *EST THIN* TO *EIN* *ITT* IS TO *E A**E TO **OTE ANOTHER'S *IT. - CHRISTIAN NESTE** *O*EE

"THE NEXT BEST THING TO BEING..."

NBL ELAN SLVN NBDEP NH SLDEP RDNNC DV NH SL WSML NH OYHNL WEHNBLF'V RDN. - IBFDVNDWE ELVNLMM SHKLL
THE NEXT BEST THING TO BEING *ITT* IS TO BE AB*E TO **OTE ANOTHER'S *IT. - CHRISTIAN NESTE** BO*EE

I've no idea who the author is yet, I hope the name will fill in. "THE NEXT BEST THING TO BEING WITTY IS TO BE ABLE TO something (QUOTE?) ANOTHER'S WIT."

NBL ELAN SLVN NBDEP NH SLDEP RDNNC DV NH SL WSML NH OYHNL WEHNBLF'V RDN. - IBFDVNDWE ELVNLMM SHKLL
THE NEXT BEST THING TO BEING WITTY IS TO BE ABLE TO **OTE ANOTHER'S WIT. - CHRISTIAN NESTELL BO*EE

OK, I'll let OYHNL=QUOTE.

NBL ELAN SLVN NBDEP NH SLDEP RDNNC DV NH SL WSML NH OYHNL WEHNBLF'V RDN. - IBFDVNDWE ELVNLMM SHKLL
THE NEXT BEST THING TO BEING WITTY IS TO BE ABLE TO QUOTE ANOTHER'S WIT. - CHRISTIAN NESTELL BO*EE

Darn, the author's last name didn't fill in, and I don't recognize his first two names. On to online search. When I started searching on "Christian Nestell" I got the suggested search term "Christian Nestell Bovee quotations". I found versions of this quote - either he paraphrased himself a couple of times, or others have paraphrased him. The idea is the same, quoting someone else's wit as a substitute of being witty yourself.

Christian Nestell Bovee was a mid-19th century American author from New York. Wikipedia doesn't seem to have an entry for him yet.
